29@format_doc(base_doc, components="", footer=doc_footer) 

30class ITRS(BaseCoordinateFrame): 

31 """ 

32 A coordinate or frame in the International Terrestrial Reference System 

33 (ITRS). This is approximately a geocentric system, although strictly it is 

34 defined by a series of reference locations near the surface of the Earth (the ITRF). 

35 For more background on the ITRS, see the references provided in the 

36 :ref:`astropy:astropy-coordinates-seealso` section of the documentation. 

37 

38 This frame also includes frames that are defined *relative* to the center of the Earth, 

39 but that are offset (in both position and velocity) from the center of the Earth. You 

40 may see such non-geocentric coordinates referred to as "topocentric". 

41 

42 Topocentric ITRS frames are convenient for observations of near Earth objects where 

43 stellar aberration is not included. One can merely subtract the observing site's 

44 EarthLocation geocentric ITRS coordinates from the object's geocentric ITRS coordinates, 

45 put the resulting vector into a topocentric ITRS frame and then transform to 

46 `~astropy.coordinates.AltAz` or `~astropy.coordinates.HADec`. The other way around is 

47 to transform an observed `~astropy.coordinates.AltAz` or `~astropy.coordinates.HADec` 

48 position to a topocentric ITRS frame and add the observing site's EarthLocation geocentric 

49 ITRS coordinates to yield the object's geocentric ITRS coordinates. 

50 

51 On the other hand, using ``transform_to`` to transform geocentric ITRS coordinates to 

52 topocentric ITRS, observed `~astropy.coordinates.AltAz`, or observed 

53 `~astropy.coordinates.HADec` coordinates includes the difference between stellar aberration 

54 from the point of view of an observer at the geocenter and stellar aberration from the 

55 point of view of an observer on the surface of the Earth. If the geocentric ITRS 

56 coordinates of the object include stellar aberration at the geocenter (e.g. certain ILRS 

57 ephemerides), then this is the way to go. 

58 

59 Note to ILRS ephemeris users: Astropy does not currently consider relativistic 

60 effects of the Earth's gravatational field. Nor do the `~astropy.coordinates.AltAz` 

61 or `~astropy.coordinates.HADec` refraction corrections compute the change in the 

62 range due to the curved path of light through the atmosphere, so Astropy is no 

63 substitute for the ILRS software in these respects. 

64 

65 """
